Overview

This project includes a collage cover of Alice in Wonderland and a personal logo.

Details & Reflections

The book cover is for the story, Alice in Wonderland. I started by finding images through royalty-free sights and brought them into Photoshop. In approaching this design, I wanted to capture the mystery, uncertainty, and creativity of the story. To create this tone, I added a vignette, a purple tint, and darkened the image using the Darkening blending mode. To mimic an ethereal look, I drew dots to imitate fireflies and colored the area around the mushroom with yellow, then used the Hard Mix blending mode. I found a font that looked fun and mysterious. I then added an oil paint filter to tie everything together. Since the story is from the 1800's, I thought the hand-drawn look would be more appropriate.

The second part of the project, the logo, represents my personal identity. I used pink since it is my favorite color but I also added black to balance the colors and add a more professional feel. There are 2 symbols I considered in this logo - the first is my heritage. I am Japanese and grew up with origami. The diagonal lines represent folded paper. The second is my ideology in design - I think good design has a strong message or strategy, is functional, and is aesthetically pleasing. The 3 triangles represent these items. Finally, if you look at the white space, it spells ‘M’, representing the first letter in my name, Melissa. To create the logo, I started by sketching the designs. I then used illustrator to draw a square and 2 diagonal lines. I separated the shapes using the Shape Builder tool, deleted the excess pieces, and colored the remaining triangles. I then added my name and colored the text to match the logo.

Both raster and vector images provide unique aesthetics and distinct advantanges in their use. Like in any design, both mediums provide a valuable way to communicate information visually.
