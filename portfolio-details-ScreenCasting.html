Overview

This screencast is a short tutorial on how to create a 3d illustration using Adobe Illustrator.

Details & Reflections

I have been using illustrator for over 6 years but the 3d materials effect is a recently updated feature and something I had not used much prior to this project. I feel that speaking is not one of my strengths and this project took me multiple times to record before publishing. Initially, I went through the steps several times and revised what I would say. I recorded through Camatasia and applied various effects such as scrolling, panning, and command annotations.

The most difficult technical aspect was the microphone. Generally, I would record through Audacity however for this project, I recorded through Camatasia. In part because of my microphone, the audio picked up a lot of white noise. While I was able to cut out large noises, such as a cough, I was not able to reduce a lot of the sound. For this reason, my recording does not sound crisp. In the future, I will explore alternative ways to record or improve narrations.

I believe screencasting is a great medium to use in educational technology. It is relatively easy to set up and provides a personal way to teach something.
